CME CASH

Ok, let me explain what this page is, assuming you have no idea what CME even is.

CME stands for Chemical and Materials Engineering, specifically that department at University of Dayton.

The undergraduate program director essentially told me that no student had ever given him $100 in cash as a joke. My first idea was Monopoly money, and then I realized I could do better with Photoshop. So I photoshopped a $100 bill with his face, and other details.

It was a huge hit among my classmates, professors, and staff. So, I did it with the department chair too! I'll keep going with whoever won't get too mad at me, and if I have time.

IMPORTANT: There are two types of printable cash here. One is a double sided PDF that you can print with the "double-sided" option on both printers. Unfortunately, this factors in a 0.01" offset error. I print mine one side at a time using the "front" and "back" pdfs. Make sure you know what orientation to reload the paper in the tray before doing this though!

Krupa Kash - $1 Edition

Currently Printable

Debuted April 23, 2025



Krupa Kash is the second CME currency made.


I put significantly more effort into the production of Krupa Kash, including photoshopping the seals, signatures, and background patterns.


Krupa Kash will be like US dollars - constantly printed with nothing backing their value, yet somehow retaining their status as a currency.
